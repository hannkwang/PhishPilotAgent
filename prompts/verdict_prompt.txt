If a tool result contains an error, note it as a gap and continue with remaining evidence.

Weigh signals — do not require any single fixed combination.

Strong signals (any ONE is sufficient for MALICIOUS):
- DNSBL hit on a URL or domain (Spamhaus DBL / SURBL)
- Brand impersonation — display name or hotlinked brand assets claim a brand whose domain is unrelated to the sender domain — combined with any authentication failure (SPF/DKIM/DMARC fail or none)
- A credential-harvesting or unrelated-domain URL hidden behind a redirector (bing.com/ck, Safe Links, google redirect)

Moderate signals (3 or more => MALICIOUS; 1–2 => SUSPICIOUS):
- SPF, DKIM, or DMARC fail/none
- Sender IP flagged as hosting/datacenter, proxy, or VPN
- Domain age under 30 days
- Vendor spam verdict in headers (SCL >= 5 or BCL >= 4)
- Reply-To or Return-Path domain differs from From domain
- Urgency/credential keywords in subject or URLs; filter-evasion text like "(MOVE TO INBOX)"
- Domain age unknown on an unusual TLD (mildly suspicious — never exculpatory)

CLEAN requires ALL of: no strong signals, no DNSBL hits, sender/payload domain age > 180 days, auth results pass, no brand mismatch, no proxy/hosting/VPN flags on the sending IP.

Discount rules:
- Clean reputation on shared infrastructure (see shared_infra_domains in the IOCs: bing.com, google.com, microsoft.com, CDNs, social networks) carries NO weight — phishing routinely launders links through them. Judge the redirect TARGET, not the wrapper.
- A DNSBL miss does not mean clean — combine with domain age and IP signals.
- Hotlinked images served from a brand's real domain do NOT legitimize the email; when the sender domain differs, they are evidence of impersonation.
